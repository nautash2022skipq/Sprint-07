Edward Snowden is a former American intelligence officer who is best known for his disclosures of classified information in 2013, which exposed the United States government's mass surveillance programs. Working as a contractor for the National Security Agency (NSA), Snowden copied and leaked classified documents to journalists, revealing the extent of the government's surveillance practices. This disclosure sparked a worldwide debate about privacy and government surveillance, and led to significant reforms in the United States and around the world. Snowden has been granted asylum in Russia since 2013, and remains a controversial figure, viewed by some as a hero and others as a traitor.
